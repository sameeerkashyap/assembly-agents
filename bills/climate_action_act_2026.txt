CLIMATE ACTION AND RENEWABLE ENERGY TRANSITION ACT 2026

SECTION 1: PREAMBLE

Whereas climate change represents an urgent threat to public health, economic stability, and environmental sustainability; and whereas transitioning to renewable energy sources is essential for reducing greenhouse gas emissions; this Act establishes a comprehensive framework for climate action and renewable energy development.

SECTION 2: EMISSION REDUCTION TARGETS

(a) The nation shall reduce carbon dioxide emissions by 50% below 2020 levels by the year 2030.

(b) A pathway to net-zero emissions shall be established by 2050.

(c) Annual progress reports shall be submitted to Parliament detailing emission reductions achieved and measures implemented.

SECTION 3: RENEWABLE ENERGY INVESTMENT

(a) Establishment of the Renewable Energy Infrastructure Fund with an initial allocation of $100 billion over 10 years.

(b) Funds shall be allocated as follows:
    - 40% for solar energy infrastructure
    - 30% for wind energy development
    - 20% for energy storage technology
    - 10% for research and development of emerging technologies

(c) Priority shall be given to projects in economically disadvantaged regions.

SECTION 4: CARBON PRICING MECHANISM

(a) A carbon tax of $50 per metric ton of CO2 equivalent shall be imposed on corporations exceeding established emission limits.

(b) The carbon tax shall increase by $10 per metric ton annually until 2035.

(c) Revenue generated shall be allocated to:
    - 50% for renewable energy projects
    - 30% for affected worker transition programs
    - 20% for climate adaptation infrastructure

SECTION 5: GREEN JOBS TRAINING PROGRAMS

(a) Establishment of the Green Workforce Development Program with $15 billion in funding over 5 years.

(b) The program shall provide:
    - Free vocational training in renewable energy sectors
    - Income support during training periods
    - Job placement assistance
    - Priority access for workers displaced from fossil fuel industries

SECTION 6: IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE

(a) Phase 1 (Year 1): Establish regulatory framework and begin infrastructure planning
(b) Phase 2 (Years 2-5): Deploy renewable energy projects and implement carbon pricing
(c) Phase 3 (Years 6-10): Scale programs and achieve interim emission targets

SECTION 7: OVERSIGHT AND ACCOUNTABILITY

(a) The Climate Action Oversight Committee shall be established to monitor implementation.

(b) Annual independent audits shall assess program effectiveness and fiscal responsibility.

(c) Public transparency portals shall provide real-time data on emissions, investments, and job creation.
